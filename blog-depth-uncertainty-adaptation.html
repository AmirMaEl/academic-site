Exploring Uncertainty and Adaptation in Depth Estimation

  • #DepthEstimation
  • #ComputerVision
  • #ResearchUpdate

This month our group wrapped two complementary projects on monocular depth estimation that move the needle on both reliability and adaptability. One investigates how to quantify prediction confidence after a model has been trained, while the other introduces a lightweight route to bridge domain gaps without extra labels.

Revisiting Gradient-Based Uncertainty

In Revisiting Gradient-Based Uncertainty for Monocular Depth Estimation, Julia Hornauer, Amir El-Ghoussani, and Vasileios Belagiannis revisit post hoc confidence scoring. We show that gradients carry rich signals even when ground-truth depth is unavailable by encouraging consistency between predictions and a pseudo-reference depth. The method improves calibration on safety-critical benchmarks with negligible overhead.

  • Benchmarks: KITTI and NYUv2 with strong gains in expected calibration error.
  • Deployment: No retraining required; works on top of existing models.
  • Read the paper: IEEE TPAMI PDF

Consistency for Unsupervised Domain Adaptation

Consistency Regularisation for Unsupervised Domain Adaptation in Monocular Depth Estimation, led by Amir El-Ghoussani, focuses on a different challenge: keeping performance stable as cameras, lighting, or environments change. We treat adaptation as a semi-supervised problem and introduce a perturbation-consistency loss that lets a single model traverse domains without elaborate training schedules.

  • Simplicity: Train one network with a pairwise consistency loss, avoiding teacher–student setups.
  • Results: State-of-the-art accuracy on KITTI ↔ NYUv2 adaptation with improved robustness in ablations.
  • Resources: arXiv abstract · GitHub code

Putting the Pieces Together

Together these studies cover both axes of dependable depth estimation: understanding when predictions might fail and adapting the model when the world shifts. Our next steps involve combining the uncertainty estimator with the domain adaptation pipeline to drive data selection, and assessing how uncertainty-aware adaptation can guide active learning.
